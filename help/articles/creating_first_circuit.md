Here is the quickest way to build and simulate your first circuit in CircuitWorkbench.

1. Tap the + button on the home screen and enter a name for your circuit.

2. The schematic editor opens. Tap Resistor in the palette and tap the canvas to place it. Place a second resistor. Place a Voltage Source (DC). Place an Earth Ground symbol.

3. Tap the Wire tool in the palette. Tap the positive terminal of the voltage source, then tap one end of the first resistor to connect them. Continue wiring: first resistor to second resistor, second resistor back to the negative terminal of the voltage source, and the ground symbol to node 0 (the bottom terminal of the voltage source).

4. Tap each component to set its value in the Properties panel at the bottom. Set the voltage source DC value to 5, the first resistor to 1k, the second resistor to 1k.

5. Tap the Run button in the toolbar. Choose Operating Point and tap Run. The results appear showing the voltage at each node and the current through each source.

That is a complete circuit simulation. From here you can explore more complex topologies, add semiconductor components with SPICE models, or try AC or transient analysis.
