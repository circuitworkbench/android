Annotations are text labels that appear next to a component on the canvas showing one of its property values. They are optional and purely informational — they do not affect the simulation.

ENABLING ANNOTATIONS
In the Properties panel, each editable field has a small checkbox on its left side. Check the box to display that field's value as a text annotation next to the component on the canvas. Uncheck to hide it.

For example, checking the Resistance checkbox on a resistor shows the resistance value (e.g. 10k) next to the resistor symbol. Checking the Model checkbox on a BJT shows the assigned model name.

WHAT GETS ANNOTATED
The component reference designator (R1, C3, Q2, etc.) is always shown and cannot be hidden. Additional annotations for value fields are opt-in via the checkboxes.

REPOSITIONING ANNOTATIONS
Tap and drag an annotation label on the canvas to reposition it relative to its component. The repositioned offset is saved with the circuit. If you move the component, the annotation moves with it maintaining the same relative offset.

TIPS
- Annotate resistance values on all resistors for a readable schematic.
- Annotate model names on semiconductors to quickly verify assignments at a glance.
- For complex circuits, be selective — annotating every field on every component produces visual clutter. Annotate only the values that matter most for understanding the schematic.
