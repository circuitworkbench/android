ZOOMING
Pinch with two fingers to zoom in and out. The canvas zooms around the midpoint between your fingers. Double-tap empty canvas to reset zoom to the default level configured in Settings.

PANNING
Drag with one finger to pan the canvas when the Select tool is active and no component is selected. If a placement tool is armed, single-finger drag pans instead of placing — this lets you navigate the canvas without accidentally placing components or cancelling the tool.

GRID
Components snap to the nearest grid intersection during placement and movement. The grid is visible as a dot pattern at normal zoom levels and hides when zoomed out far enough that dots would overlap. Grid spacing and snap behaviour can be adjusted in Settings under Grid Settings.

TIPS
- Zoom in close when placing components that need to connect to specific pins of other components — the snap points become easier to hit accurately.
- Zoom out to get an overview of a large schematic and check for disconnected wires or misplaced components.
- The canvas has no fixed boundary — you can spread a large circuit across as much space as needed.
